# Street-Guide-Map

This project empowered visitors and NYSC members with maps utilizing mobile GIS to map Google Maps’ blind spots. It became the official template for GOGIS, streamlining state land administration and earning me the CCAO Climate Change Ambassador title


## Aim
The aim was to eliminate navigational blind spots in Gombe Metropolis, where global platforms like Google Maps failed. By leveraging mobile GIS and community volunteers, I created a reliable spatial framework for visitors and NYSC members, ensuring they could navigate the city safely and efficiently.

Beyond navigation, the project aimed to provide the Office of the Surveyor General and GOGIS with a localized, high-resolution digital template. This professional-grade mapping was designed to modernize land administration, improve urban planning accuracy, and demonstrate how low-cost geospatial technology could bridge the data gap in Nigerian urban centers.


## Resource used
* QGIS
* Mobile GIS
* Microsoft Office suite
* Government records

## Significance
It's my first innovative, real-life application of GIS in providing solutions (Post undergraduate).
The project’s significance lies in its role as the architectural blueprint for Gombe State’s modern land administration. By mapping Google Maps blind spots, local navigation was transformed for thousands of visitors and NYSC members, enhancing both safety and accessibility.

Beyond navigation, it provided the Gombe Geographic Information System (GOGIS) with a foundational dataset for urban planning and street naming. This initiative proved that volunteer-led mobile GIS is a viable, low-cost solution for data-sparse regions. Ultimately, the project’s success elevated Ipede to a CCAO Climate Change Ambassador, highlighting the critical link between spatial data and sustainable urban resilience.
